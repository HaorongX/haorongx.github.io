### My CS Life

I started my CS(not Counter-Strike, but computer science) life in elementary school. I always have fun with computer programs, it's my biggest hobby so far. It's just an amazing feeling to build a huge machine by writing code, I have full control of the entire thing, and watch it from start to over. Later I got interested in [PostgreSQL](https://postgresql.org). I heard about this DBMS by chance. It was when I tried to build my website or something, I compared several mainstream DBMS(mainly MySQL and SQLite because I had no money to buy Oracle or MS SQL Server). But eventually I found a DBMS called Postgres which was not that famous but pretty powerful. I used it right away and it actually performed very well(even better than MySQL, I daresay). And then I couldn't bear to just see Postgres as a blackbox anymore, I decided to dive into the kernel of it.

### Kernel Diver

Kernel is a scary word to most beginners, even experienced developers. But the kernel is the best part of any system. Let's take Postgres for example. You can do many cool things with it, but you are still just using it as a user. You put your input in, and Postgres returns something. You never know what happened during this process. What did it do during the one second I waited? This question brings me go through the user interface and down to the kernel. Well, it is complicated. However, we can use some tools to cut the kernel pieces and learn it step by step.

### Why Do I Create This Blog

While I'm inspired by many great documentations, a fact really worries me. That is, there isn't such a document which is suitable for a beginner(I mean people who knows nothing about Postgres Kernel) to get started. There isn't a document that explains the kernel from its design to the source code, either. But such a document is especially precious for beginners. As a person who is curious about Postgres and a beginner who knows nothing, I decide I must do it myself.
