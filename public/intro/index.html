Introduction

PostgreSQL, as the most advanced open source relational database, has released the 16th version on November 9, 2023. As a developer who had been interested in Postgres for so long, I was always seeking a chance to dive into the kernel of Postgres. However, I found that although the official document and some books give us a fairly general view of the internals of Postgres, there’s no such a document which explains the entire system from the top to the bottom. When I say “top” and “bottom”, actually they refer to the abstraction and the source code, respectively.

This fact makes it pretty hard to understand the source code. What’s worse, Postgres itself is complicated enough to bring up confusion. So that led me to the conclusion that maybe I should write such a document.

In the following posts, I’ll take you through the design and the source code of PostgreSQL. Our journey begins at the most complicated subsystem – Query processing.
